## Describe the task

<!-- para:0004 -->
Claude brings deep knowledge of job markets, industry trajectories, and skill trends that aren't obvious from job postings alone. Paired with your resume and goals, this expands your career horizons while developing the strategic thinking skills to navigate future transitions.

<!-- para:0005 -->
Tell Claude about your career goals and provide your resume—it will map out a strategic path forward.

<!-- para:0006 -->
I'm a sophomore at UC Berkeley targeting these roles:

<!-- para:0007 -->
- Associate Product Manager at a music streaming company - [https://musicstreaming.com/careers/apm](https://musicstreaming.com/careers/apm)
- Product Designer at a design collaboration platform - [https://designplatform.com/careers/designer](https://designplatform.com/careers/designer)
- UX Researcher at a travel platform - [https://travelplatform.com/careers/researcher](https://travelplatform.com/careers/researcher)

<!-- para:0008 -->
After analyzing my resume against these roles, build:

<!-- para:0009 -->
**Action Tracker (Google Doc): ** Forward-looking planner showing what to do next. Include skill gaps with projects to build, timeline with semester/year/grad milestones, networking targets, learning roadmap, and local resources. Tight spacing, scannable, built for weekly planning.**

<!-- para:0010 -->
**Skills Portfolio Log (Excel): ** Backward-looking evidence bank showing what I've done. Log completed projects with metrics, skills inventory with proof points, quantified achievements, learning completions, and STAR stories tagged by competency. Built for mining resume bullets and cover letters.**

## Optional context

<!-- para:0017 -->

Make sure [Web Search](https://support.claude.com/en/articles/10684626-enabling-and-using-web-search) is enabled so Claude can search the web for current information.

## Follow up prompts

<!-- para:0028 -->
### Prepare for coffee chats

<!-- para:0029 -->
Come prepared with informed questions and connect your experience to what matters for that conversation. Ask Claude to help research the role context, company, and industry.

<!-- para:0030 -->
I'm meeting a product manager from the streaming company next week. Research the company's product philosophy and competitive positioning, and then create a prep sheet with questions to help me understand their challenges, as well as aspects of my background that are important to highlight.

<!-- para:0031 -->
### Draft differentiated application materials

<!-- para:0032 -->
Claude can teach you how to adapt your materials for different roles and what makes them effective.

<!-- para:0033 -->
Using my portfolio log, show me how to adapt my resume for PM, design, and research roles—what should I emphasize for each?

<!-- para:0034 -->
### Update with new goals

<!-- para:0035 -->
Claude can revise your career plan when priorities shift or new opportunities emerge, maintaining the structure while adapting content to changed circumstances.

<!-- para:0036 -->
I just discovered UX writing roles that combine my interests. Can you analyze this job posting and update my action plan to include this career path?

## Tricks, tips, and troubleshooting

<!-- para:0038 -->
### Understand AI's role in your target career

<!-- para:0039 -->
While exploring your career path, learn how AI is already reshaping that field. The [Anthropic Economic Index](https://www.anthropic.com/economic-index#job-explorer) lets you see AI adoption patterns across hundreds of occupations—revealing whether people in your target roles are using AI to augment their work (collaborating with AI as a thinking partner) or automate tasks (delegating routine work entirely).

<!-- para:0040 -->
Understanding this distinction helps you build the right skills: roles heavy on augmentation require strong judgment and creative direction, while automation-focused work demands technical fluency in managing AI systems. Use the job explorer to investigate your target occupations, then ask Claude to help you identify which skills will matter most as AI transforms the field.

<!-- para:0041 -->
### Use styles in your career conversations

<!-- para:0042 -->
Create a custom [style](https://support.claude.com/en/articles/10181068-configuring-and-using-styles) to shape how Claude engages with your questions. For example, set a style that responds like a career counselor—when you express doubts, Claude asks clarifying questions; when you propose ideas, Claude helps you think through trade-offs. This back-and-forth refines both the plan and your own thinking. Go to "Use style" in the search and tools menu, then "Create & edit styles" to describe the kind of conversational partner you need for career decisions.

<!-- para:0043 -->
### Track your ongoing career work

<!-- para:0044 -->
Create a [Project](https://support.claude.com/en/articles/9517075-what-are-projects) and upload your resume, action tracker, and skills log once to the Project Knowledge. Every conversation in that Project automatically has access to everything—you won't need to re-upload or re-explain context. Claude's [Memory](https://support.claude.com/en/articles/11817273-using-claude-s-chat-search-and-memory-to-build-on-previous-context) feature allows it to learn from your conversations within a project, remembering your career priorities, target roles, and preferences to build upon previous discussions.

<!-- para:0045 -->
### Research your target roles and companies

<!-- para:0046 -->
Use [Web Search](https://support.claude.com/en/articles/10684626-enabling-and-using-web-search) when you need quick, specific answers to ground your planning—like details on target companies, recent industry events, or potential skill-building programs. For more in-depth analysis, enable [Research](https://support.claude.com/en/articles/11088861-using-research-on-claude) in the chat settings. Rather than piecing together information yourself, Research searches across your tools and the web to deliver a thorough report with source citations.
