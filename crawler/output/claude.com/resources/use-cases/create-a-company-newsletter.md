## Describe the task

<!-- para:0004 -->
Claude can read across your company channels, identify what matters to you, and package it into a publication-style digest. You get curated company intelligence delivered in a format you'll actually want to read. Tell Claude which week to summarize and point to the channels or topics that matter most. Ask for an interactive publication with data visualizations showing data and trends.

<!-- para:0005 -->
Create a weekly digest for Oct 14-20 for my company.

<!-- para:0006 -->
Pull information from relevant channels in my Slack, such as #company-announcements, #product-updates, #leadership, and any other channels where major decisions happened.

<!-- para:0007 -->
Include data visualizations where useful. Use a dense but readable layout. Pack information efficiently and use sophisticated typography and spacing.

<!-- para:0008 -->
Go for a British newspaper aesthetic: no gradients, shadows, rounded corners, or bright colors. Print-like typography with tight leading. Include institutional publishing elements—standfirsts, pull quotes with left borders, desk attributions, sourced charts using Chart.js. This should look hand-crafted by an editorial designer. Include substantive developments formatted like a real publication I'd read on Sunday evening.

## Give Claude context

<!-- para:0011 -->
To create your digest, Claude needs access to your workspaces. Claude can read across all your channels to identify which updates are important to include. For a more complete view, add additional sources where company information that you want to include lives.

## Tricks, tips, and troubleshooting

<!-- para:0032 -->
### **Use prompting techniques to trigger specific designs**

<!-- para:0033 -->
The prompt includes phrases like "Print-like typography" or "Institutional publishing elements". These descriptions help inspire Claude to design and format your information in a way that mimics digital publications and online news sites. Without them, Claude may default to more basic formatting.

<!-- para:0034 -->
### **Publish and share the artifact**

<!-- para:0035 -->
After Claude creates an artifact, [publish and share it](https://support.claude.com/en/articles/9547008-discovering-publishing-customizing-and-sharing-artifacts) through the URL. Others can open it in the browser, without needing a Claude account. If someone requests changes, you can ask Claude to update the artifact and republish to display the new version.

<!-- para:0036 -->
### ‍**Tune preferences over time and create skills**

<!-- para:0037 -->
**‍**As you discover how you like your content formatted and which channels for sourcing information work best, give Claude feedback and ask to create a [Skill](https://support.claude.com/en/articles/12512176-what-are-skills). Learn more about how to [create a custom skill using Claude.](https://support.claude.com/en/articles/12599426-how-to-create-a-skill-with-claude-through-conversation)**
