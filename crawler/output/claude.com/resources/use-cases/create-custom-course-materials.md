## Describe the task

<!-- para:0004 -->
Claude can process visual content and translate mathematical notation into proper La TeX syntax, which allows you to focus on refining pedagogy rather than wrestling with typesetting commands and box formatting.

<!-- para:0005 -->
Describe the kind of course materials you want Claude to create using your handwritten notes and course objectives.

<!-- para:0006 -->
I'm a math professor converting my handwritten integration notes into a professional course materials. I have:

<!-- para:0007 -->
- Handwritten notes with equations
- A typed outline of topics

<!-- para:0008 -->
Craft a professional-looking La TeX document, resembling a published textbook page with colored boxes and precise equations, then convert it to PDF.

## Follow up prompts

<!-- para:0024 -->
### Enhance visual design

<!-- para:0025 -->
Add additional design elements beyond the basics—margin notes, visual hierarchies, call-out boxes. Claude applies design thinking to make content more functional and engaging.

<!-- para:0026 -->
Add margin notes with key insights, a visual table of contents at the chapter start, and use different shading for 'Quick Check' boxes where students can test understanding before moving forward.

<!-- para:0027 -->
### Generate matching problem sets

<!-- para:0028 -->
Create practice materials that complement educational content. Claude generates exercises organized by difficulty with appropriate scaffolding or solution frameworks.

<!-- para:0029 -->
Create a separate homework assignment document with 15 integration by parts problems organized by difficulty. Include worked solution templates showing the u and dv selection, but leave the actual integration steps blank for students to complete.

<!-- para:0030 -->
## Tricks, tips, and troubleshooting

<!-- para:0031 -->
### Explicitly specify certain visual elements

<!-- para:0032 -->
For a polished, publisher-quality look, tell Claude to use the tcolorbox package for colored theorem and example boxes, instead of standard La TeX rectangles. The package gives you control over colors, borders, rounded corners, and spacing that makes materials look professionally typeset.

<!-- para:0033 -->
### Activate design excellence through framing

<!-- para:0034 -->
Use specific prompting techniques to avoid default outputs. Instead of "create a La TeX document," say "design this like a premium calculus textbook from a major publisher" or "match the visual sophistication of Stewart's Calculus."

<!-- para:0035 -->
### Use projects for consistency across chapters

<!-- para:0036 -->
If you're converting multiple chapters, create a [Project](https://support.claude.com/en/articles/9517075-what-are-projects) that maintains context across conversations. Claude remembers your visual preferences, theorem numbering scheme, color choices, and formatting standards.
